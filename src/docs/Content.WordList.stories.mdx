When writing about Palmetto products/platform, it’s important to be consistent with capitalization so that we can reinforce the uniqueness of these offerings.

- "Ambassador" should be capitalized when talking about our Ambassador Program or Ambassadors.
- “Program” must be written with a capital P when referencing the Ambassador Program
- “Mapdwell” has no capital letters other than the ‘M’.
- “Palmetto App” should use the capital A to identify it as a specific product.
- Palmetto App should always be referred to as “Palmetto App” or “the Palmetto App”, not App.


If a product or feature is unique to Palmetto, capitalize it. If isn't unique, don't capitalize it.

## Words To Use Carefully

We use plain language, which means avoiding industry jargon. But some jargon-adjacent words can be appropriate in educational contexts. Only use these if you’re writing about solar education and have room to briefly define them.

- buyer journey
- conversion
- customer journey
- customer lifecycle
- integrated marketing
- lead generation
- marketing funnel
- multichannel marketing
- omnichannel marketing
- product-market fit
- value proposition
- permission to operate

## Words To Avoid

- funnel, incentivize, leverage, disruption, thought leader, learnings, or other fluffy corporate terms
- internets, interwebs, or any other variation of the word “internet”
- ninja, rockstar, wizard, unicorn (unless referring to a literal ninja, rockstar, wizard, or unicorn)
- young, old, elderly, or any other word describing a person's age
- crushing it, killing it, killer, awesome
- crazy, insane, or similar words to describe people
- best-in-breed
- Silicon Valley cliches like rise and grind, or disruptor/disruption
- activation (when referring to our presence at an event)
